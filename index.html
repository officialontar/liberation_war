Introduction

36th July" is a popular culture reference to August 5, 2024, the day of the mass uprising that led to the resignation of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina and the end of the July Revolution in Bangladesh. The non-existent date signifies the protesters' belief that the protests of July would not end until victory was achieved. The July Revolution began as a quota reform movement in June 2024 but grew into a nationwide uprising marked by violent suppression, culminating in Hasina's removal from power and the subsequent establishment of an interim government led by Muhammad Yunus.
